CFO REAFFIRMS ITS OUTSTANDING GENDER-RESPONSIVE STATURE WITH BACK-TO-BACK GADTIMPALA AWARDS

Secretary Romulo V. Arugay receives the back-to-back GADtimpala Awards of the Commission on Filipinos Overseas and expressed his elation at the recognition as symbols of the Commission’s progressive efforts in mainstreaming gender and development in its programs and services for overseas Filipinos.

A first in its history, the Commission on Filipinos Overseas was awarded back-to-back GADtimpala Awards on August 14, 2023 by the Philippine Commission on Women during the 14th Anniversary Celebration of the Magna Carta of Women (MCW) at the Samsung Hall of the SM Aura, BGC, Taguig City.

The GADtimpala, which stands for Gender and Development Transformation & Institutionalization through Mainstreaming of Programs, Agenda, Linkages & Advocacies, is the official awards system of the PCW honoring government agencies and local government units for outstanding performance in gender mainstreaming and implementation of gender-responsive programs.

Under the leadership of Secretary Romulo V. Arugay, the CFO was conferred with the GADtimpala SILVER for Outstanding Gender-Responsive Agency for the agency’s outstanding performance in gender mainstreaming and the effective implementation of gender-responsive programs, and GADtimpala SILVER for Exemplary GAD Focal Point System.

The elevation of the agency’s bronze award in the 2018 GADtimpala to silver this year is a validation of the agency’s progressive performance in gender mainstreaming, particularly in advocating for the protection and promotion of the welfare of overseas Filipinos.

The conferment of the Silver Award for Exemplary GFPS in the PCW’s first GADtimpala Awards System for the GFPS to CFO GAD Focal Point System (GFPS) highlights the dedication and commitment of the members of the CFO-GFPS in continuously taking bold strides in reviewing, developing, implementing, and promoting the Commission’s gender and development initiatives.

The two (2) awards validate the CFO’s exemplary performance in gender mainstreaming. In his acceptance speech, Secretary Arugay thanked the PCW for recognizing the Commission’s progressive efforts in mainstreaming gender and development in its policies and programs. He added that it was an honor to be conferred these awards alongside other government agencies, with whom the CFO shares the same aspiration of concretizing the impacts of upholding women’s rights, achieving gender equality and empowerment, transforming people’s lives regardless of gender identity and expression, and strongly reaffirming the crucial role of women in nation-building.

Secretary Arugay poses together with the CFO GAD Focal Point System whom he regarded as the real partners in attaining a holistic gender-responsive agency for overseas Filipinos.

In time for the 14th anniversary since the Magna Carta of Women was enacted into law, Secretary Arugay expressed that it is a suitable occasion to look back and assess the significant milestones of all the agencies and offices to evolve and innovate effective and responsive gender equality programs and advocacies.

Secretary Arugay accepts the GADtimpala Trophy, designed by artist Julie Luch, and the glass plaque from the Philippine Commission on Women and candidly remarked that these awards, alongside the 17 other government agencies, represent the Commission’s aspiration of transforming people’s lives regardless of their gender identity and expression.

He also reaffirmed the Commission’s commitment to closing the gender gap within the agency and for the clientele it serves.

Secretary Arugay hoped that beyond the sworn compliance of the government agencies to statutory requirements of gender mainstreaming in the bureaucracy, all fellow government agencies and public servants may continue to be the catalysts of change in upholding equal and equitable rights and opportunities for the Filipino people, wherever they may be.
